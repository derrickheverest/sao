Feedback Overview

There are three different roles that can help offer feedback. These roles usually should not be done together. Instead, you should focus on one of these roles and fulfill it to the best of your ability. The three roles are as follows:

  1. The Ah-Counter
  2. The Grammarian
  3. The Evaluator

The Ah counter focuses on individual words. It is picky about filler words and likes to point out repetitive words and on occasion, misspelled words

The Grammarian does not look at inidivial words. Instead, he is concerned with finding Gems and Germs. A Gem can be Something that sounds quotable. It can be a part or several parts that were written beautifully. A Germ can be a part that was offensive. A Germ can be a gramatically INCORRECT sentence.

The Evaluator focuses on content. He focuses on structure. Each story should have a clear structure to it. The evaluator looks for 3 pieces of content that he enjoyed and 2 parts that could use some improvement.

Scheduled plan

  1. two-minute game plus 3 minutes of The Ah-counter (repeat for one week
  2. two-minute game plus 3 minutes of the Grammarian (repeat for one week
  3. two_minute game plus 5 minutes of the Evaluator (repeat for one week
  4. Transition to Writing Game Level 2
