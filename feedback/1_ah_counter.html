The Ah-Counter Role

The purpose of the Ah-Counter is to count the number of filler words. Imagine an entree that you ordered from a restaurant. You take one bite of the food and it tastes really plain. You take another bite and suddenly it is super salty! yuck! Then you take another bite and it is revoltingly sweet. The problem? The the lack of mixing and possible an over dose

The Ah-counter should count these as filler words:

  • and
  • so
  • like
  • ya know
  • basically
  • uh
  • um

However, keep in mind that SOME filler words are neccesary. You should only count it as a filler word when the use of the word does not help add meaning or if the use is repetitive; think about that over-seasoned entree.

Lastly, the Ah-Counter should count words that are being used way to much. The english language is full up many words that mean the same thing. Use this to your advantage. Go grab a thesaurus!

Examples

  • I went to the store and walked down the aisle and picked up cereal because basically it was on sale.
    • I went to the store, walked down the aisle and picked up cereal because it was on sale.
  • And it was um, uh, like totally awesome!
    • It was incredibly awesome!
  • Her beautiful face caught my attention. Her beauty was all I could think about. Her beautiful eyes. Beautiful skin. Beautiful hair.
    • Her beautiful face caught my attention. Her exotic nature flooded my mind. Her eyes sparkled like diamonds. Her skin shimmered. Her hair, flowed like fine silk.
